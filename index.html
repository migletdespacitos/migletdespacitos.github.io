About

I'm Miguel, a final-year Data Science & Analytics student at NUS with a strong interest in machine learning and quantitative finance. My work spans regime-adaptive deep hedging frameworks, AI tooling for government workflows, credit risk modelling, and bioinformatics classification — reflecting a broad technical foundation I apply wherever data and rigour matter.

I'm drawn to problems where financial mathematics and ML converge — options pricing, risk modelling, portfolio construction — but I thrive equally in other data-rich environments. Past work has taken me into public health policy, genomics, and operational research, and I'm comfortable adapting my toolkit to new domains and problem structures.

Whether the context is a trading desk, a policy division, or a research lab, the goal is the same: make sense of complex data and act on it well.

Outside of work, I enjoy chess, basketball, cycling, guitar, cooking, and following Formula 1.

Languages: English, Filipino, Mandarin (Intermediate), French (Elementary).
